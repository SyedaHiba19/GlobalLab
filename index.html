PFT

Pulmonary function tests are a group of tests that measure how well the lungs take in and release air and how well they move gases such as oxygen from the atmosphere into the body’s circulation.



ECG

An electrocardiogram (ECG) is a test that checks how your heart is functioning by measuring the electrical activity of the heart. With each heart beat, an electrical impulse (or wave) travels through your heart. This wave causes the muscle to squeeze and pump blood from the heart.



Sonography 4D

In ultrasonography, or ultrasound, high-frequency sound waves, inaudible to the human ear, are transmitted through body tissues. The echoes are recorded and transformed into video or photographic images.



Digital X RAY

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Do I need fasting for investigations?

    Yes, certain investigations do need fasting status. The most common
    investigations which require fasting include :-
    • • Lipid Profile – 12 Hours
    • • Blood Sugar Fasting – 10 to 12 Hours
    • • Insulin Fasting – 10 to 12 Hours
    • • C Peptide Fasting – 8 to 10 Hours
    • • Ultrasound – Whole abdomen, Upper abdomen
    • • TMT
    Common investigations which do not require fasting include:-
    • • Kidney Function Test (Urea, Creatinine, Uric Acid, Electrolytes)
    • • Liver Function Test (Bilirubin Total, Direct, SGOT, SGPT, Alkaline Phosphatase, GGTP)
    • • Thyroid Function Test
    • • HbA1c
    • • Urine Culture
    • • ECG It is always advisable to talk to the lab In-charge and clarify in advance.

  • How do I prepare for an Ultrasound Examination?
